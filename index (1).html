Sheet A–001 — Profile

Twelve years at the drafting table.

Elias Vance is an independent architectural draft engineer producing construction documentation, structural detailing, and BIM coordination for architecture firms, structural engineers, and general contractors across residential, commercial, and civil work.

Trained on the board before moving fully digital, every set still gets a hand-scale check before it goes out the door — because a drawing that reads clean on screen can still be wrong at 1/4" = 1'-0".

Fluent in the handoff between design intent and buildable detail: reading redlines, resolving clashes, and keeping revision sets tight through permit and RFI cycles.

Sheet A–005 — Process

From sketch to issued set.

The same five-step sequence runs through every project, scaled to the size of the scope.

01

Intake & Redlines

Receive concept sketches, architect markups, or field notes and confirm scope, scale, and deliverable format.

02

Modeling

Build the base model or drawing in Revit or AutoCAD at true scale, structured for the sheets to come.

03

Detailing & Coordination

Resolve conflicts across disciplines, add dimensions and annotation, and detail every assembly called out.

04

QA Check-Set

Plot and hand-check every sheet against the specification before anything is marked ready to issue.

05

Issue & Revise

Deliver a stamped-ready set and track revisions cleanly through permit review and RFI cycles.
